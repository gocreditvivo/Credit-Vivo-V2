Customer Disclosures

Last updated: June 21, 2026

Launch note: These disclosures are working drafts for the MVP. Final customer-facing language, cancellation forms, payment timing, and state-specific notices should be reviewed by counsel before Credit Vivo accepts payment, pulls credit reports, or submits disputes.

Credit Repair Organizations Act Notice

You have the right to dispute inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable information in your credit report directly with a credit bureau or furnisher. You may do this yourself without hiring Credit Vivo.

Credit Vivo cannot legally remove accurate, current, and verifiable information from a credit report just because it is negative. Credit Vivo does not guarantee any score increase, tradeline deletion, loan approval, credit-card approval, settlement result, or specific timeline.

If the Credit Repair Organizations Act applies, Credit Vivo must provide required disclosures before a covered credit repair agreement is signed. Credit Vivo should not collect payment for covered credit repair services before those services are fully performed.

FCRA Rights

You have rights under the Fair Credit Reporting Act, including the right to dispute incomplete or inaccurate credit-report information, provide supporting documents, receive investigation results, and request correction or deletion of information that cannot be verified.

Credit Vivo may help organize report data, prepare customer-approved dispute language, track responses, and explain next steps. Credit Vivo does not decide what a credit bureau, furnisher, lender, court, regulator, or collection agency will do.

Three-Business-Day Cancellation

If your service is covered by CROA, you may cancel your credit repair contract without penalty within three business days after signing. The final production checkout should include a separate cancellation notice and a simple way to cancel.

Credit Pull Authorization

Before Credit Vivo pulls or connects to a credit report, you must authorize Credit Vivo and its selected service provider to access your credit-report data for the services you requested. A credit pull should not happen until identity verification, privacy consent, FCRA acknowledgment, and credit-pull authorization are complete.

Dispute Authorization

Before Credit Vivo sends disputes or validation requests, you must authorize Credit Vivo to prepare and submit customer-approved communications to credit bureaus, furnishers, collectors, complaint portals, and other permitted recipients. You are responsible for providing true and complete information.

Payment And Billing

Credit Vivo may collect payment-method information only through the selected payment processor. If CROA applies to a service, covered fees should not be charged before the covered work is fully performed. Subscription, monitoring, education, document storage, or non-CROA services may require separate review and clear service descriptions.

AI Credit Review And Internal Forensic Scanner

Credit Vivo may use software and AI-assisted analysis to organize reports, identify possible inconsistencies, prioritize review, and prepare plain-language recommendations. AI output is not a credit decision, legal opinion, financial guarantee, or substitute for customer-provided records.

The internal forensic scanner, Metro 2/FCRA issue scoring, furnisher license workbox, and raw backend analysis are not shown in the customer dashboard. Customers see understandable findings, document requests, dispute status, and recommended next steps.

The chat assistant is designed to help customers navigate the portal and understand general next steps. It should not provide legal advice, promise credit-score increases, guarantee deletions, or display raw backend scanner output.

Attorney Escalation

Attorney backup or escalation is not automatic legal representation. Any attorney consultation, letter, review, or representation must follow applicable attorney-service terms, authorization requirements, and conflict checks. Credit Vivo should not use an attorney's name, signature, or letterhead unless that attorney has approved the specific use.

Privacy And GLBA Notice

Credit reports, identity documents, addresses, account numbers, payment details, and dispute records are sensitive. Credit Vivo should collect only what is needed, protect it with reasonable safeguards, limit access, and provide required privacy notices, including the GLBA Privacy Notice where applicable.

SMS, Email, And Review Requests

Credit Vivo may send service updates, document reminders, dispute-status messages, and review requests only where the customer has provided the required consent. Customers must be able to opt out of marketing or review-request messages.

The secure portal should remain the source of truth for full updates. Email and SMS alerts should be short notifications that ask the customer to sign in, without exposing account numbers, creditor names, balances, dispute allegations, or other sensitive credit-report details.

Credit Vivo may ask customers for honest feedback. Credit Vivo does not offer incentives for reviews, ask for a specific rating, gate review requests based on satisfaction, or post reviews for customers.

State-Specific Notices

Credit repair, collection, telemarketing, privacy, cancellation, licensing, and attorney-service rules may vary by state. Before launch, Credit Vivo should add any state-specific notices required for the customer's state and Credit Vivo's operating state.
