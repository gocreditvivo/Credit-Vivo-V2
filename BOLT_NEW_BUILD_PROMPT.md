# BOLT.NEW BUILD PROMPT — CREDIT VIVO PUBLIC WEBSITE

You are an expert web designer and frontend engineer. Rebuild and visually improve the Credit Vivo public website using the existing content provided in this project.

## Brand
Name: Credit Vivo
Slogan: You take control. We clear the path.

## Goal
Create a polished, calm, trustworthy public website for a consumer credit improvement platform. The content is already good; the main task is visual design, spacing, images/illustrations, layout balance, and professional polish.

## Inspiration
Use the visual balance of calm service websites such as Sky Blue Credit, Dovly, Credit Saint, and CreditRepair.com, but do NOT copy their layout, images, branding, wording, icons, or exact structure.

The site should feel:
- Calm
- Trustworthy
- Clean
- Professional
- Easy to read
- Less text-heavy
- Not loud
- Not oversized
- Not like a crypto/app launch page
- More like a polished consumer financial service website

## Important Visual Direction
The current content is text-heavy. Add original visual balance using:
- Soft abstract credit-roadmap illustrations
- Friendly dashboard preview cards
- Simple progress tracker mockups
- Credit check-in card
- Roadmap timeline
- Secure document/upload illustration
- Learning-center preview card
- Optional support card
- Calm customer-service imagery style

Do not use copyrighted competitor screenshots.
Do not use images from Dovly, Sky Blue, Credit Saint, Lexington Law, or CreditRepair.com.
Use original illustrations, simple shapes, cards, icons, and soft gradients.

## Typography
The previous version had fonts too large. Keep typography calm:
- Hero headline: about 38–46px desktop, 30–34px mobile
- Section headings: about 28–36px desktop
- Body text: 15–17px
- Card headings: 17–21px
- Buttons: 14–15px
- Use comfortable line height
- Avoid extremely bold, oversized text

Do NOT put Free Scan or Findings in public top nav. Those are member/private pages.

## Bottom Buttons Rule
At the bottom of each public page, show helpful next-step buttons, but do NOT repeat the current page.
Example:
- On Why Credit Vivo page, do not show a Why Credit Vivo button at the bottom.
- On Pricing page, do not show a Pricing button at the bottom.
- On FAQ page, do not show an FAQ button at the bottom.
- On Learning page, do not show a Learning button at the bottom.
- On Join Free page, do not repeat Join Free at the bottom.
