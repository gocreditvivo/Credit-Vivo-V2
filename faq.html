Frequently Asked Questions

Last updated: June 21, 2026

Plain-English support: These answers explain the Credit Vivo MVP. Final billing, cancellation, attorney-service, state-specific, and credit-pull language should be reviewed before launch.

What does Credit Vivo do?

Credit Vivo helps customers organize credit reports, identify possible reporting issues, prepare customer-approved disputes, store documents, and track responses from credit bureaus and furnishers.

Can I dispute credit report errors myself?

Yes. You can dispute inaccurate or incomplete information directly with the credit bureaus and furnishers for free. Credit Vivo is an optional support service.

Can Credit Vivo guarantee removals?

No. Credit Vivo does not guarantee removals, score increases, credit approvals, or specific outcomes.

Does Credit Vivo remove accurate information?

No. Accurate, current, and verifiable information cannot legally be removed just because it is negative.

Will customers see the forensic scanner?

No. The forensic scanner is an internal backend tool. Customers see plain-language findings, recommended next steps, document requests, and dispute status.

Do you pull credit reports?

The MVP is prepared for a credit-report provider connection. Before any pull, the customer must complete identity verification, privacy consent, FCRA acknowledgment, and credit-pull authorization.

Is the credit pull a hard inquiry?

Credit Vivo should connect to a soft-pull credit-report provider for this workflow. The final provider must confirm inquiry type before launch.

When can Credit Vivo charge customers?

Payment timing depends on the service and applicable law. If CROA applies, covered credit repair fees should not be charged before the covered work is fully performed.

How much does Credit Vivo cost?

The launch draft uses a free Credit Snapshot, Vivo Starter at $29/month, Vivo Plus at $59/month, and Vivo Advanced + Attorney Backup at $99/month after eligible work where required.

Why is Credit Vivo priced lower than many competitors?

Credit Vivo keeps the portal simple, uses backend automation carefully, avoids showing raw scanner logs to customers, and offers a no-setup-fee launch structure while still keeping attorney escalation in the top tier.

How long do disputes take?

Credit bureau investigations often take about 30 days, but timing can vary based on the dispute, documents, and applicable rules.

What happens when responses arrive?

Customers can upload bureau or furnisher responses to the Document Vault. Credit Vivo then tracks the result and recommends the next customer-approved step.

Can Credit Vivo text or email me when there is an update?

Yes, if you consent. Text and email alerts should stay short and direct you to sign in to the portal for details. Sensitive account details should stay inside the portal.

What can the chat assistant help with?

The chat assistant can help customers find portal updates, upload documents, understand general dispute workflow, learn credit basics, and navigate to the right section. It does not give legal advice, promise results, or show raw backend scanner data.

What is attorney escalation?

Attorney escalation is a top-tier route for issues that may need legal review. It is not automatic representation and must follow separate attorney-service terms.

Can Credit Vivo file CFPB or state complaints?

Credit Vivo can help organize complaint packets where appropriate, but a complaint does not guarantee a correction, payment, deletion, or legal outcome.

Can I opt out of messages or review requests?

Yes. Customers should be able to opt out of marketing or review-request messages. Review requests must ask for honest feedback only.
