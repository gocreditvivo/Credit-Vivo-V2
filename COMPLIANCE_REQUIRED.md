# Credit Vivo Required Compliance Checklist

This checklist is for launch readiness and does not replace attorney review.

### Required Public Facing Elements

- [ ] **The "DIY" Right:** The site must perpetually display the federal right to dispute inaccuracies directly with bureaus for free.
- [ ] **No Advance Fees for Services:** If the platform ever transitions from a SaaS subscription to a "pay-per-deletion" model, CROA prohibits charging advance fees before the service is fully rendered.
- [ ] **3-Day Right to Cancel:** The Terms of Service must prominently display the consumer's right to cancel their subscription within 3 business days of signing up without penalty.
- [ ] **No Outcome Guarantees:** Marketing copy must never use words like "guarantee," "erase," or "instant" regarding credit score increases or the removal of legitimate negative tradelines.

- CROA/FCRA style disclosures: no guaranteed removals or score increases, right to dispute directly at no cost, cancellation/refund terms, written service agreement before paid services.
- Privacy and GLBA-style safeguards: collect only needed data, protect credit reports and IDs, limit staff access, keep vendor agreements, document retention and deletion.
- Electronic consent: customer agrees to portal, email, and SMS updates before messages are sent.
- Payments: disclose monthly fees, setup fees, certified mail/postage, credit monitoring, identity verification, attorney review, and third-party report access separately unless included.
- Attorney-assisted services: do not imply legal representation unless a separate attorney-client relationship is created.
- State launch controls: verify Virginia, Maryland, and DC credit services rules, business registration, bond/license needs, and cancellation language before accepting paid customers.
